I'm a second year student pursuing my Masters in Computer Science at the University of Southern California. My research interests are in the application of Sequence to Sequence models to the task of Language Generation. I got my first exposure to these areas when I joined the Information Sciences Institute of the University of Southern California as a Student Research Worker.

I have been working under the guidance of Prof. Premkumar Natarajan for over a year now. Currently, I am a summer intern at ISI where I am working on the task of Neural Abstract Generation using Sequence to Sequence Modelling. The idea is given a title as an input along with some additional context, the system should output a well formed, coherent, diverse abstract. Right now, the project focuses on the scientific domain but the applications of such a system can be enormous.

Prior to joining USC for my Masters, I worked as an Infrastructure Developer at Hike Messenger, one of the largest consumer tech companies of India. As a member of the infrastructure team, I familiaried myself with a vast array of distributed systems and related technologies working at scale. I developed and worked on a a bunch of microservices and was a part of some major refactorings of critical system components.

My work experience has helped me in understanding the intricacies involved in developing critical and time-intensive software that supports millions of users on a daily basis and my research experience is providing me in-depth knowledge of Deep Learning and its applications to Natural Language Understanding and Generation.
